Volunteer

Welcome to the Cornwall branch of Butterfly Conservation,
dedicated to saving butterflies, moths and our environment.

Become a volunteer

Join the team

Cornwall Butterfly Conservation is managed by a group of friendly, motivated and experienced volunteers, eager to share their knowledge of the butterflies and moths that you can see in Cornwall. We are always keen to enlist new recruits, so, if you would like to get involved, you’ll be sure of a warm welcome.

How you can help

Join Butterfly Conservation, if you are not a member already (ref webpage). If you join the Branch Committee, you get your first year’s subscription free.

Submit ‘casual’ records of butterflies and moths that you see on a walk, in your garden, or anywhere in the County. If you have a smart phone, it’s really easy with the iRecord Butterflies app – available from both Google Play and iTunes and helps you to identify the butterfly you have seen. You can choose what suits you best from a menu of recording schemes here. Some people prefer to jot down details in a notebook and send them to the County Butterfly Recorder: there is online guide to help you.

Join the UK Butterfly Monitoring Scheme by contributing observations in a more structured way. Several options are described here and involve a commitment to survey somewhere you can reach easily and regularly. Recording a transect might entail as few as four walks a year (during the flight season of one of our rarer species) or as much as a weekly walk throughout the summer. You will get plenty of support and help to get started, and you will become a member of a county network providing vital data. (Please contact the Transect Co-ordinator: ref CONTACTS)

Help with habitat management and surveys (ref webpage). You will join a group of dedicated volunteers who enjoy working out of doors throughout the year. It might involve clearing scrub to allow vital larval foodplants to flourish or counting webs of caterpillars on moorland. You can choose to join in as many or as few events as you want. No previous experience or knowledge is necessary: information and training will be provided on the day. Stout boots and all-weather wear are the only necessities! (Please contact the Volunteer Co-ordinator: ref CONTACTS)

Join the Branch Committee to help us run the branch and its activities. Perhaps you have skills and experience that will be useful to us, in say, organisation, leadership, communication or learning, for example. A specialised knowledge of Lepidoptera is NOT necessary, and you get a year’s free membership of Butterfly Conservation! (Please contact the Chair: ref CONTACTS)

Cornwall Butterfly Conservation welcomes Georgina Paul to the role of Volunteer Coordinator.

Georgina, who likes to be called Georgie, was selected from a strong field in early April. The role is the only paid one in the branch and is funded from money raised by the branch.

Georgie will work for CBC 30 days in the year, and will fit this role around her many other commitments. As CBC Volunteer Coordinator, she will organise, lead and manage field work, which will include surveys of some of our rarer butterfly species, habitat management, public engagement and fund-raising. As the title of her job implies, her work will revolve around and depend upon CBC’s amazing band of volunteers, who are ready and raring to go after the last two years’ Covid restrictions.

Put Our Team To Work

CBC charges £250 (+ VAT) per day to put it's volunteer workforce at the service of other organisations. This includes all costs, such as preparation, organisation, delivery supervision, travel and insurance. CBC is a branch of the national charity, Butterfly Conservation. It is managed by volunteers, who are not professional ecological consultants but do draw on extensive experience of the Lepidoptera of Cornwall, their life cycles and and their varied habitats. CBC welcomes requests for work that will contribute to the conservation and knowledge of butterflies in Cornwall. Our work is aligned with the aims, standards and conservation priorities of Butterfly Conservation. If you would like to discuss your requirements, please contact Jim Cooper via email or 01872 560710 or 07968 515651
